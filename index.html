Identify your IP
TRL 1–2 · Basic research → concept formulation
▼
⚠ Protect before you publish. Consult WIC before thesis submissions, conference presentations or any publications. A provisional patent or embargo must be in place first — once published, novelty is lost.

Patents

Protect inventions. File a local provisional patent first, then pursue international registration. Wits (via WIC) covers filing costs.

Copyright

Covers software, creative and written works. Arises automatically on creation — register with WIC to formalise ownership.

Trade marks

Protect brand name, product name or logo. Register via WIC before any public disclosure or go-to-market activity.

Know-how

Unpatentable methods, algorithms, software processes. IP may be ceded or licensed to your spinout by agreement with WIC.

Who owns the IP? By default, Wits owns IP created by staff and students using institutional resources. The IPR Act (2008) requires all publicly-funded IP to be disclosed to the institution. Wits may license or cede IP to a spinout, or license it on arm's-length terms to a third party.

2
Stress-test your idea
TRL 3–5 · Proof of concept → lab validation
▼

WIC assesses five readiness dimensions before recommending next steps:

Technology Readiness

TRL 1–9 scale (TIA). Basic research → system proven in operational environment. TIA funding accessible from TRL 3 upward.

Market Readiness

MRL 1–9. Is there a validated gap? Have you spoken to potential customers or end-users?

Manufacturing Readiness

Can your solution be produced at viable scale and cost? Lab proof ≠ manufacturable product.

Investment Readiness

Business model canvas + market validation required before approaching any funders or investors.

IP Readiness

Protection must be in place before any public disclosure, investor pitch or commercial conversation.

4
Form your spinout
TRL 7–8 · Investment-ready · BRL 6–8
▼

1. Register idea

Via WIC at any stage of your research journey. Early registration protects you and triggers WIC support.

2. IP strategy

WIC files provisional patent or other protection. No investor conversations before this step.

3. Agree equity terms

Standard Terms document available. Wits minimum share is 9%, maximum 24%, based on cumulative contributions.

5
Grow your venture
TRL 8–9 · Full commercial deployment · BRL 7–9
▼

Incubate at Tshimologong

Coworking, MakerSpace, IBM Research Lab, mentorship, digital skills programmes. Equity or fee arrangement (~10%).

Accelerate via Wits Crucible

ECDP → formal incubation pathway → acceleration for top performers. 47 Main St, Joburg CBD. 250-entrepreneur cohort planned for 2026.

Wits Innovation Fund (WIF)

Investment vehicle for Wits spinouts. Subject to WIF committee approval. Separate from WIC pre-seed support.

⚠ Ongoing governance obligations. Disclose all directorships annually and within 30 days of any change. Declare and recuse from conflicts of interest. Comply with Wits IP Policy at all times. Academic inventors should sit on the board — not in the C-Suite. Use of the Wits brand requires prior written approval.
